The Real Difference Between Turnkey Construction and Conventional Contracting

By Fusion 4 Construction Experts
March 20, 2024
Construction Methods, Project Management
7 min read
Understanding the right approach for your construction project

When starting a construction project, one of the most important decisions is choosing how the project will be executed. Two commonly followed approaches in the construction industry are Turnkey Construction and Conventional Contracting.

While both methods aim to deliver a completed building, the way responsibilities, coordination, and risks are handled is very different. Understanding this difference helps clients make informed decisions and avoid unnecessary stress during execution.

Conventional Contracting

Multiple Partners

Split responsibility model with client as central coordinator between multiple contractors and consultants.

Turnkey Construction

Single Source

Integrated approach with one company responsible for end-to-end execution and delivery.

Method 1

What Is Conventional Contracting?

In conventional contracting, the construction process is split among multiple parties. The client typically appoints:

Architect/Designer

For design and drawings

Civil Contractor

For structure and shell work

Specialized Vendors

For interiors, finishes, services

Method 2

What Is Turnkey Construction?

Turnkey construction follows a single-point responsibility model. One company manages the entire project from start to finish â€” including design coordination, approvals, construction, services, and final handover.

The client receives a ready-to-use building, hence the term "turnkey".

Key Differences Explained Simply

Side-by-side comparison for clear understanding

1

Responsibility & Accountability

Conventional Contracting

Responsibility is shared across multiple parties, which can lead to confusion when issues arise.

Turnkey Construction

One team is fully accountable, making problem-solving faster and clearer.

2

Coordination & Communication

Conventional Contracting

The client coordinates between architects, contractors, and vendors.

Turnkey Construction

All coordination is handled internally by the turnkey partner.

3

Time & Schedule Control

Conventional Contracting

Delays are common due to dependency between multiple vendors.

Turnkey Construction

Integrated planning allows better timeline control and predictable delivery.

4

Cost Management

Conventional Contracting

Costs may increase due to design gaps, changes, or rework.

Turnkey Construction

Budgets are planned holistically, reducing unexpected expenses.

5

Quality Consistency

Conventional Contracting

Quality varies depending on individual contractors.

Turnkey Construction

Centralised supervision ensures consistent quality standards.

6

Client Stress & Involvement

Conventional Contracting

Requires continuous involvement and decision-making.

Turnkey Construction

Offers a stress-free experience with structured reporting.

Which Approach Is Right for You?

Matching project requirements with the right construction method

Conventional Contracting

Best for
  • Small projects
  • Clients with construction experience
  • Projects needing frequent design changes
  • Budget-focused projects with flexibility needs

Turnkey Construction

Ideal for
  • Residential, commercial, and large-scale projects
  • Clients seeking peace of mind
  • Projects with defined budgets and timelines
  • Busy professionals without time for coordination

Quick Decision Matrix

Consideration Conventional Contracting Turnkey Construction
Time Availability High involvement required Minimal involvement needed
Technical Knowledge Helpful to have Not required
Risk Tolerance Higher (manages multiple contracts) Lower (single point responsibility)
Budget Predictability Variable (potential for cost overruns) Fixed (better cost control)

Why Turnkey Construction Is Gaining Preference

As projects become more complex, clients increasingly prefer turnkey models because they offer clear accountability, better control over timelines and costs, reduced coordination risks, and predictable project outcomes. Turnkey construction allows clients to focus on the result, while execution is handled professionally.

Our Commitment to You

Our goal is to ensure clients experience clarity, confidence, and consistency throughout the project lifecycle. Whether you choose conventional contracting or turnkey construction, understanding these differences empowers you to make the right choice for your project.
